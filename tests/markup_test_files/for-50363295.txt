===Etymology===
{{PIE root|en|preh₂}}
From {{inh|en|enm|for}}, from {{inh|en|ang|for||for, on account of, for the sake of, through, because of, owing to, from, by reason of, as to, in order to}}, from {{inh|en|gem-pro|*furi||for}}, from {{der|en|ine-pro|*preh₂-}}. Cognate with {{m|fy|foar||for}}, {{cog|nl|voor||for}}, {{cog|de|für||for}}, {{cog|da|for||for}}, {{cog|sv|för||for}}, {{cog|no|for||for}}, {{cog|is|fyrir||for}}, {{cog|la|per||by, through, for, by means of}} and Romance language successors (e.g. {{cog|es|para||for}}), {{cog|grc|περί||for, about, toward}}, {{cog|lt|per||by, through, during}}, {{cog|sa|परि||over, around|tr=pári}}.

===Preposition===
{{en-prep}}

# [[towards|Towards]].
#: ''The astronauts headed '''for''' the moon.''
# Directed at, [[intended]] to [[belong]] to.
#: ''I have something '''for''' you.''
# Supporting (opposite of ''against'').
#: ''All those '''for''' the motion raise your hands.''
# [[because of|Because of]].
#: ''He wouldn't apologize; and just '''for''' that, she refused to help him.''
#: {{qualifier|UK usage}} ''He looks better '''for''' having lost weight.''
#: ''She was the worse '''for''' drink.''
#* Shakespeare
#*: with fiery eyes sparkling '''for''' very wrath
#* '''1867''', Frederick Metcalfe, ''The Oxonian in Iceland'' (page 202)
#*: "A summerly day for you," said my host; "You ought to be here in winter. It is impossible then to get out of the doors '''for''' the snow and wind. Ugh! dreadful weather!"
# Over a period of time.
#: ''I've lived here '''for''' three years. ''
#: ''They fought '''for''' days over a silly pencil.''
#* Garth
#*: To guide the sun's bright chariot '''for''' a day.
# Throughout an extent of space.
#* Shakespeare
#*: '''For''' many miles about / There's scarce a bush.
# On behalf of.
#: ''I will stand in '''for''' him.''
# Instead of, or in place of.
#* Bible, Exodus xxi. 23, 24
#*: And if any mischief follow, then thou shalt give life '''for''' life, eye '''for''' eye, tooth '''for''' tooth, hand '''for''' hand, foot '''for''' foot.
# In order to [[obtain]] or [[acquire]].
#: ''I am aiming '''for''' completion by the end of business Thursday.''
#: ''He's going '''for''' his doctorate.''
#: ''Do you want to go '''for''' coffee?''
#: ''People all over Greece looked to Delphi '''for''' answers.''
#: ''Can you go to the store '''for''' some eggs?''
#: ''I'm saving up '''for''' a car.''
#: ''Don't wait '''for''' an answer.''
#: ''What did he ask you '''for'''?''
#* Denham
#*: He writes not '''for''' money, nor '''for''' praise.
# In the [[direction]] of: {{non-gloss definition|marks a [[point]] one is going toward.}}
#: ''Run [[for]] the hills!''
#: ''He was headed '''for''' the door when he remembered.''
#* Francis Bacon
#*: We sailed from Peru '''for''' China and Japan.
# By the [[standard]]s of, usually with the implication of those standards being lower than one might otherwise expect.
#: ''Fair '''for''' its day.''
#: ''She's spry '''for''' an old lady.''
# [[despite|Despite]], [[in spite of]].
#* '''1892''' August 6, [[w:Charles Dickens|Charles Dickens]], "The Unbidden Guest", in ''All the Year Round'',<sup >[http://books.google.com/books?id=XNwRAAAAYAAJ ]</sup> [http://books.google.com/books?id=XNwRAAAAYAAJ&amp;pg=PA133&amp;dq=%22but+for%22 page 133],
#*: Mr. Joseph Blenkinshaw was perhaps not worth quite so much as was reported; but '''for''' all that he was a very wealthy man {{...}}
#* '''1968''', J. J. Scarisbrick, ''Henry VIII'' (page 240)
#*: '''For''' all his faults, there had been something lofty and great about him - as a judge, as a patron of education, as a builder, as an international figure.
# {{non-gloss definition|Used to indicate the [[subject]] of a [[to-infinitive]].}}
#: '''''For''' that to happen now is incredibly unlikely.'' (=''It is incredibly unlikely that that will happen now.'')
#: ''All I want is '''for''' you to be happy.'' (=''All I want is that you be happy.'')
# {{lb|en|chiefly|US}} [[out of|Out of]]; {{non-gloss definition|used to indicate a fraction, a ratio}}
#: {{ux|en|In term of base hits<!--see base hit-->, Jones was three '''for''' four on the day}}
# {{lb|en|cricket}} {{non-gloss definition|used as part of a score to indicate the number of wickets that have fallen}}
#: {{ux|en|At close of play, England were 305 '''for''' 3.}}
# Indicating that in the character of or as being which anything is regarded or treated; to be, or as being.
#* Cowley
#*: We take a falling meteor '''for''' a star.
#* John Locke
#*: If a man can be fully assured of anything '''for''' a truth, without having examined, what is there that he may not embrace '''for''' true?
#* Dryden
#*: Most of our ingenious young men take up some cry'd-up English poet '''for''' their model.
#* Philips
#*: But let her go '''for''' an ungrateful woman.
# {{non-gloss definition|Used to construe various verbs.}} See the entry for the phrasal verb.
# {{lb|en|obsolete}} Indicating that in prevention of which, or through fear of which, anything is done.
#* Beaumont and Fletcher
#*: We'll have a bib, '''for''' spoiling of thy doublet.
