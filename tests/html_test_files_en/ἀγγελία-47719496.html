Ancient Greek

Alternative forms

  • ἀγγελίη (angelíē) – Ionic, Epic

Etymology

From ἄγγελος (ángelos, “messenger”) +‎ -ῐ́ᾱ (-íā, abstract noun suffix).

Pronunciation

 
  • IPA(key): /aŋ.ɡe.lí.aː/ → /aŋ.ɡeˈli.a/ → /aɲ.ɟeˈli.a/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /aŋ.ɡe.lí.aː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /aŋ.ɡɛˈli.a/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aŋ.ɡeˈli.a/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aɲ.ɟeˈli.a/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aɲ.ɟeˈli.a/

Noun

ἀγγελῐ́ᾱ • (angelíā) f (genitive ἀγγελῐ́ᾱς); first declension

  1. message, news, report
  2. Also, the substance or means of such communication
  3. instruction, command
